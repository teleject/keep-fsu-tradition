Drop the FSU "Wal-Mart Logo"

What's Happening with the FSU Logo?

FSU Athletics Department initiated a change to streamline the FSU Chief's logo so that it could be reproduced better at smaller sizes.

Who Created the Logo?

FSU Athletics Dept. reached out to NIKE for input in creating the new FSU logo.

At this time, no one or set of individuals have claimed ownership or management of the new logo project other than FSU Athletics Department and, in their official statment, NIKE.

When Did This FSU Logo Occur?

The logo has been under development for two years, according to a statement by FSU Athletics.

Why Change the FSU Logo?

A "modern" logo allowa FSU and NIKE to display the logo "more prominently across all platforms including our uniforms, sideline apparel and graphics".

How Did the Public Learn about the New FSU Logo?

Apparently this will be FSU's new logo. This shirt was spotted at Walmart.. pic.twitter.com/KFFsR24882

— Mike Yazigi (@Mike_Yazigi94) April 2, 2014

Someone saw the new logo on a t-shirt in a Wal-Mart and tweeted it, thus earning the nickname "Wal-Mart Logo".

Was the FSU Community Asked for Their Input into the New "Wal-Mart Logo"?

No.

While FSU has asked for public input on simpler things like license plate redesigns, they did not ask for public input or public submissions to update the FSU logo.

Why Didn't FSU Athletics Ask the FSU Community for Input?

That's the multi-million dollar marketing and merchandising question.

You'd think asking the public to take part in the new logo's development would get the community to not only adopt the new logo and actually buy more merchandise since they would have had some part in ownership in its creation.

Have New Logos from the Community Been Developed?

In response to the "Wal-Mart Logo" by NIKE, at least two fan-generated professional logos have been created:

Jodi Slane's Logo

FSU alumnus and artist Jodi Slade created a new logo that meets the stated goals of FSU Athletics, but also keeps the visual identity of the original logo intact.

rentz's Logo

On a Sports Logo forum, member "rentz" submitted a new design that's fresh in its direction and keeps in tune with the original logo.

These are only two fan or alumni generated logos. One can only imagine the amount of originality that would be tapped if FSU Athletics asked the FSU Community for its input.

How are These Logos Better than the "Wal-Mart Logo"?

According to FSU Athletics' own statement, the rationale behind the logo change was to "does not reproduce well in a number of mediums".

  1. The "Wal-Mart Logo" has the letters "FSU" in very small print. At small sizes, those letters will not render in smaller printings. In essence, FSU Athletics stepped into the same problem they say they were trying to get out of.
  2. The goal of the redesign for the logo was also to bring back the Chief as symbol. However, by placing the feather as gold on the "Wal-Mart Logo", they make the feather the more striking feature.
  3. The face for "Wal-Mart Logo" appears more "comic book" or "cartoonish" making it a stylistic departure from the previous illustration. This goofish approach has lead to many sight gags on Twitter.

Simply put: the "Wal-Mart Logo" fails to meet FSU Athletics' own stated goals for a modern re-imagination of the logo.
